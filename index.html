Heads up — under 2 years of history, annualized figures can look absurd. The math is correct; extrapolating a short, sharp run as a steady yearly rate usually isn't.

Lump-sum CAGR is what the same total dollars would have returned invested all at once on day one instead of spread out $1/day — it's the stock's own buy-and-hold return, shown here for comparison against the strategy above.

Past performance doesn't predict the future. Survivorship bias is real — this only works on tickers that survived. The boring lesson is the gap between the two lines.
